Dear Brothers and Sisters:
Easter Season Seminary Reverence Initiative
After prayerful consideration with the First Presidency and Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, we are pleased to announce a special "Easter Season Reverence Initiative" for all seminary programs throughout the Church beginning April 2nd through April 18th, 2025.
As we commemorate our Savior's infinite Atonement and glorious Resurrection during this sacred Easter season, we invite all seminary students to demonstrate increased reverence by attending daily seminary classes in Sunday worship attire. The First Presidency has long taught that our outward appearance can reflect our inward commitment to honor sacred spaces and experiences.
This temporary dress standard for seminary attendance includes:
  • Young women: Modest dresses or skirts appropriate for sacrament meeting
  • Young men: White shirts, ties, and dress slacks (suit coats are encouraged but optional)
This initiative aligns with our recent emphasis on creating reverent learning environments and will help our youth:
  1. Experience a deeper connection to the sacred events we commemorate at Easter
  2. Practice the principle that reverent appearance invites reverent behavior
  3. Prepare more fully for future temple and missionary service
Stake Presidents and Bishops have been notified of this initiative. We express gratitude in advance for your faithful support in implementing this special opportunity to honor the Savior during this Easter season.
Sincerely yours,
P.S. Please note the date of this announcement carefully... April Fools! Regular seminary dress standards remain in effect. Thank you for your good humor and dedication to seminary. Perhaps this can be a springboard for a meaningful discussion about how our reverence for the Savior is shown in ways beyond our outward appearance.
